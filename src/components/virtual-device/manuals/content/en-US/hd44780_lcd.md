Use this device to observe writes on an HD44780-compatible character LCD bus and reconstruct the written characters as text.

## Supported connection modes

- `4-bit` mode: `RS / E / RW / D[7:4]`
- `8-bit` mode: `RS / E / RW / D[7:0]`

The bus-mode parameter must match the way your design is wired.

## How text reconstruction works

- A write is latched on the observed edge of `E`.
- `RS=0` is interpreted as a command, and `RS=1` as character data.
- In `4-bit` mode, two nibbles are combined into one full byte.
- The resulting DDRAM contents are mapped back into a multi-line text display.

## Current modeling focus

- The write path is the main focus.
- `RW` read cycles do not drive busy-flag or data responses back into the FPGA as a real LCD would.
- This makes it well suited for verifying written text, but not for validating busy-flag-based read timing.

## Good for debugging

- Whether the initialization sequence is correct
- Whether cursor moves, clear operations, and line changes behave as expected
- Whether a custom text-output controller writes the correct timing and data
